About GPA Calculator

Our free GPA calculator is the most accurate and easy-to-use tool for students to calculate grade point average online. Whether you're a high school student, college student, or university student, this calculator helps you determine your academic performance quickly and accurately.

What is GPA? Grade Point Average (GPA) is a numerical representation of your academic performance, calculated by averaging the grade points you earned in all your courses, weighted by credit hours. Most educational institutions in the United States use either a 4.0 GPA scale (unweighted) or 5.0 GPA scale (weighted for honors and AP classes).

How to use this GPA calculator: Simply select your grading scale (4.0 or 5.0), enter your course names, letter grades (A+, A, A-, B+, B, etc.), and credit hours. Our calculator instantly computes your GPA, showing you total grade points, credits earned, and a detailed grade distribution breakdown.

This tool supports semester GPA calculation, cumulative GPA calculation, weighted GPA for honors courses, and helps you understand how each course impacts your overall academic standing. Perfect for planning your studies, college applications, scholarship requirements, or graduate school admissions.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is GPA calculated?

GPA is calculated by multiplying each course's grade points by its credit hours, summing all grade points, and dividing by the total credit hours. For example: if you got an A (4.0) in a 3-credit course and a B (3.0) in a 4-credit course, your GPA would be (4.0×3 + 3.0×4) / (3+4) = 3.43.

What's the difference between 4.0 and 5.0 scale?

The 4.0 scale is the standard GPA scale where A=4.0, B=3.0, C=2.0, etc. The 5.0 scale is weighted and used for honors or AP classes, where A=5.0, B=4.0, allowing students to achieve GPAs above 4.0 when taking advanced courses.

What is a good GPA?

A GPA of 3.0 or above (B average) is generally considered good. A 3.5-4.0 GPA is excellent and competitive for most universities. However, "good" depends on your goals - some competitive programs may require 3.7+, while others may accept 2.5+.

Can I calculate cumulative GPA?

Yes! Enter all courses from all semesters to calculate your cumulative GPA. Make sure to include the correct credit hours for each course. The calculator will sum all grade points and credits to give you your overall GPA.

How do credit hours affect GPA?

Credit hours act as weights in GPA calculation. A 4-credit course has more impact on your GPA than a 1-credit course. Higher credit courses with good grades boost your GPA more, while poor grades in high-credit courses lower it more significantly.

Is this calculator accurate?

Yes, our calculator uses standard GPA calculation formulas. However, some schools may use different grading scales or rounding methods. Always verify with your institution's official GPA policy for the most accurate results.
