Frequently Asked Questions

How does the plagiarism checker work?
Our tool analyzes your text using AI algorithms to detect similarities with existing content. It compares phrases and patterns, highlights matched sections, and provides a percentage score indicating how much of your content may be duplicated.
Is this plagiarism checker completely free?
Yes, 100% free forever. No registration required, no credit card needed, no hidden fees. You can check unlimited content with no restrictions or premium upgrades.
How accurate is the plagiarism detection?
Our AI-powered algorithm is highly accurate at detecting exact matches and similar phrases. However, it's important to review flagged content manually, as some similarities may be common phrases or properly cited quotations. Always use this tool as a guide, not a final verdict.
Can I use this for academic papers and essays?
Absolutely! This tool is perfect for students checking essays, research papers, assignments, dissertations, and any academic writing before submission. It helps ensure your work is original and properly cited.
Is my content stored or shared?
No. We take privacy seriously. Your content is analyzed in your browser and is not stored on our servers, shared with third parties, or added to any database. Your text remains completely confidential.
What percentage is considered plagiarized?
Generally, 0-10% is considered acceptable (mostly common phrases), 10-25% requires review and revision, and above 25% indicates significant plagiarism concerns. However, standards vary by institution and context. Always check your specific requirements.

Tips for Using Plagiarism Checker

1 Check Early and Often

Don't wait until the last minute. Check your work throughout the writing process to catch and fix issues early.

2 Review Highlighted Sections

Manually review all flagged content. Some matches may be common phrases or properly cited quotations.

3 Cite Sources Properly

Always cite your sources correctly using the required format (APA, MLA, Chicago, etc.) to avoid unintentional plagiarism.

4 Paraphrase Effectively

When paraphrasing, change both the words and the sentence structure. Don't just replace words with synonyms.
